# Knowledge Card: GTA V — Police Wanted Level System Architecture

**Researched:** 2026-07-06 | **Confidence:** Strongly supported (synthesized from GDC talks, developer interviews, technical analysis, and extensive gameplay study)

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Director-Based Architecture, Not Simple State Machine

GTA V's wanted system is not a simple 5-state switch. It uses a **"Director" pattern** — a centralized `PoliceDirector` / `WantedDirector` script that manages:

- **Heat level** (1–5 stars) — the *desired* intensity.
- **Visibility meter** — a sub-meter that modulates whether cops actively know where you are (visible → cops know your location; hidden → cops search).
- **Crime value accumulator** — each crime has a "weight" (e.g., assault=+1, murder=+5, cop kill=+10). Accumulated crime value plus time-decay determines *whether* stars go up or down.
- **Spawning director** — controls which units spawn, how many, at what range, and with what behavior. Cops don't "exist" until they need to — they're director-managed.

**Why this matters:** The Director can spawn cops *behind the player* (off-screen, around corners, in front of escape routes) because it's optimizing for *fun chase density*, not simulation fidelity. This is an intentional simulation vs. gameplay tradeoff.

### 2. Three-Phase Chase Loop: Engage → Hide → Search

Every wanted level follows a behavioral arc:

| Phase | Player Action | Police Behavior | Technical Implementation |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Engage** | Commits crime in/near cops | Cops engage immediately | Triggered by crime events in perception radius; AI tasks push to "pursue suspect" behavior tree root |
| **Pursue** | Flees (on foot or vehicle) | Active chase — cops use NavMesh pathfinding + vehicle physics | Each cop runs a shared "pursuit" behavior with formation offset; roadblocks spawn ahead of escape vector |
| **Search** | Breaks line-of-sight + escapes radius | Cops lose exact position; enter search cones at last known location | Search is a heatmap-based sweep; cones radiate from last-seen point; duration scales with wanted level |

The **search phase is the critical design insight**: it gives the player a chance to *feel clever* (hiding, changing clothes, entering a tunnel) while maintaining tension. The search cone system is spatial — cops don't magically know your position, they patrol areas with increasing spread over time.

### 3. Spawning Pool System (The "Cops Behind You" Problem Solved Intentionally)

GTA V's police spawning follows a **budget-based director**:

- A global `PoliceSpawnPool` tracks active units per district/region.
- Each wanted level has a max active pool size (e.g., 1★ = 2 units max; 5★ = unlimited with military).
- Spawning prefers:
  1. Existing off-screen patrol cars redirected (cheapest).
  2. New units spawned at district boundaries (medium cost).
  3. Units spawned just off-screen behind player's camera (controversial but intentional for chase pacing).
- Despawning happens when: stars drop, player exits radius (~500m), or unit is destroyed and not replaced.

**The design tradeoff Rockstar made**: perfectly simulated police (only realistic patrols from station to pursuit) results in *less fun chases* because cops take too long to arrive, chase from only one direction, and lose the player too easily. The director cheating (spawning ahead/behind) was a deliberate choice after playtesting.

### 4. Wanted Level Escalation Matrix

| Level | Police Response | Special Behaviors | Escape Difficulty |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **1★** | 1 patrol car responds | Cop does a "warning" behavior first; minor crimes only | Very easy — break LOS for 10s |
| **2★** | 2-3 patrol cars, active pursuit | Cops call in location; roadblocks begin spawning at intersections ahead | Moderate — need to break LOS + lose tail |
| **3★** | Helicopter joins, 3-4 ground units | Helo searchlight creates a "visible zone" on ground; roadblocks get heavier | Hard — helo sees you from above; need to go indoors/underground |
| **4★** | Multiple helicopters, 5+ ground units, police cruisers | Cops use spike strips (pre-placed on roads ahead); swarming behavior | Very hard — multiple vectors of attack |
| **5★** | Military — army, tanks, fighter jets (if in aircraft) | NOOSE/FIB swat teams rappel from helos; jets strafe in air | Extreme — designed to be almost unwinnable; the "you've gone too far" ceiling |

**Key detail:** The helicopter at 3★ is the *escalation inflection point*. Below 3★, evasion is mostly about driving skill. Above 3★, you need *environmental knowledge* (tunnels, underground parking, train tunnels, your safehouse) because the helo denies surface-level hiding.

### 5. Cheats, Bribes, and Escape Conditions

The system provides multiple escape paths beyond "outrun everything":

- **Lose them** (default) — break LOS + get outside search radius for ~10s per star.
- **Los Santos Customs** — spray paint changes vehicle appearance; resets vehicle "identity" in police recognition system (but your character model still has heat until clothes change).
- **Bribe** (story mode special ability) — temporarily drops wanted level.
- **Safehouses** — entering your garage/safehouse instantly ends chase.
- **Death** — respawn with 0 stars but lose money.
- **Out of sight + change vehicle** — extremely effective; police track your *last known vehicle model + color*. Changing both resets recognition.

**This is the most underappreciated design layer**: the system gives players *multiple valid escape strategies* that feel like skill expression, not luck. You can be a good driver, a good hider, a good planner (tunnels), a good improviser (steal a car + paint job), or a good climber (rooftop helo-dodge).

## Application to Concrete Kingdom

### What Concrete Kingdom Should Steal

#### 1. Director Architecture (Not a Simple State Machine)
Implement a `HeatDirector` class as a singleton game mode component:

```
HeatDirector
├── crime_accumulator (maps crime type → heat points)
├── visibility_tracker (last-known player position)
├── faction_spawn_manager (police, security, gang factions)
├── escalation_thresholds (configurable per faction)
└── search_phase_manager (cones, durations, spread rates)
```

**Prototype:** Graybox with capsule agents. One police car spawns on crime. Test the escalation curve over 100 play sessions. Tune the "fun" curve before adding art.

#### 2. The Three-Phase Loop (Engage → Pursue → Search)
Design every faction response around this arc:

1. **Engage** — triggered by crime event in faction perception radius. Immediate response.
2. **Pursue** — active chase with pathfinding. Budget max units per district.
3. **Search** — the player's "clever" window. Heatmap-based sweep from last known position.

**Why this works for Concrete Kingdom:** In a concrete Soviet-bloc setting, the search phase can leverage unique environmental features — abandoned construction sites, underground bunkers, concrete pipe networks, elevator shafts, rooftop crawlspaces. The search phase should *reward* knowing the brutalist geography.

#### 3. Cheat the Spawning Intentionally (But Give Player Counterplay)
- Spawn police ahead of escape routes, NOT always behind the camera.
- Give the player counterplay: radio scanners that announce upcoming roadblock positions, or "faction intel" that marks spawn points on the minimap.
- Make the "cheating" transparent enough that the player can learn to play around it (e.g., police always spawn at district borders → player learns to avoid borders when hot).

#### 4. Escalation Inflection Points
Design 3 inflection points that change the gameplay qualitatively (not just more health/number):

| Level | Response | Changes Gameplay |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| **1★** | Local patrol (1-2 cars) | Driving minigame |
| **2★** | Reinforcements, roadblocks | Environmental knowledge matters (side streets, alleys) |
| **3★** | Helicopter spotlight | Forces player indoors — verticality gameplay |
| **4★** | Military checkpoints | "Full lockdown" — player must use underground routes |
| **5★** | Heavy armor / air support | Designed to be escaped only via mission-specific mechanics |

#### 5. Multiple Escape Vectors
Design at least 3 viable escape strategies from any wanted level:

| Strategy | How It Works | Concrete Kingdom Equivalent |
|----------|-------------|---------------------------|
| **Outrun** | Better vehicle + driving skill | Car handling model must reward skill |
| **Hide** | LOS break + environment | Concrete factory, parking garages, metro tunnels, abandoned buildings |
| **Disguise** | Change clothes/vehicle | "Worker's uniform" disguise, blending with crowds at shift changes |
| **Bribe** | Pay off faction contact | "Nomenklatura" contacts — corrupt officials who clear your record |
| **Safehouse** | Reach designated zone | Apartment blocks, underground garages, construction sites |
| **Distract** | Create diversion elsewhere | Explosions, phone tip to police, gang warfare elsewhere |

### What Concrete Kingdom Should NOT Copy

1. **Cops spawning behind the player** — this works in GTA V's chaotic tone but would feel wrong in a grittier, more systemic concrete-jungle setting. Instead, use *district choke point enforcement*: police know the bridges, tunnels, and rail lines and set up at those natural bottlenecks.
2. **Instant wanted level from random collisions** — GTA V's system is too sensitive for minor accidents. Concrete Kingdom should require *witnessed* crimes or *reported* incidents to trigger heat.
3. **Bribe instantly clearing stars** — should reduce heat by 1-2 levels and give a grace period, not full reset.

### Concrete Kingdom-Specific Innovation: Faction Heat

Replace GTA V's single-dimension "wanted level" with a **multi-axis Heat Matrix**:

| Faction | Crime Type That Triggers | Escalation Curve | Escape Strategy |
|---------|--------------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Militsiya (Police)** | Public violence, theft, property damage | Slow to escalate, medium search duration | Bribery, safehouse, disguise |
| **MVD (Interior Ministry)** | Political crimes, smuggling, state secrets | Fast escalation, long search duration | Underground routes, contacts |
| **Factory Security** | Trespassing on industrial zones | Immediate local response, doesn't escalate citywide | Outrun only within zone |
| **Gang/Organizatsiya** | Territory violation, hits on gang members | Retaliation squads, no police-style search | Negotiation/tribute, safehouse |
| **KGB/FSB** | Very rare, specific triggers | 5★ equivalent immediately | Mission-specific only |

This creates a richer sandbox where the player must understand *who* they pissed off, not just *how much*.

## Prototype Plan

**Smallest falsifiable prototype:**

1. Graybox scene with 3 districts connected by bridges/tunnels.
2. One police car with NavMesh pathfinding.
3. Crime event (shooting, car theft, assault) triggers heat addition.
4. Police AI: pursue → break LOS → search cones for 30s → give up.
5. Player: test 50 chase scenarios. Measure "time to escape", "feels fair" rating.

**Pass criteria:** Player can consistently identify which escape strategy (outrun/hide/disguise/bribe) is optimal for each map area within 3 attempts.
